My Profile

I partook in three online surveys to collect information on my personality and learning style. The three tests I took were:

  • Myers-Briggs Type Indicator located at https://www.16personalities.com
  • Considered by Psychology Today[1] to have more accuracy and growing support than the Myers-Briggs test, a HEXACO personality inventory, located at http://hexaco.org
  • Although learning style tests have their critics[2], the prominent VARK learning style test at http://vark-learn.com

Conclusions

The test results produced what I had already known about myself, quiet, sociable, deterministic, equal parts process, and intuition, occasionally stubborn but generally thoroughly researched, with low emotional reactivity except where it counts. In a team setting, I am conscious of not dominating discussion, process, or outcomes, and try to strike a balance between occasional guidance and frequent collaboration. I enjoy working with people who can balance ambition and opinions with the needs of the team; many voices often lead to the best solutions.

VARK learning style test

The VARK learning style test classified me as a multimodal learner, preferring a range of styles rather than one, trending towards visual and kinaesthetic learning. My result seems to reflect reality, where I will often watch online courses and then work through additional examples to cement the learning. I will resort to white papers, documentation, and articles where I must, however, prefer the ‚Äúwatch then do and repeat‚Äù way of learning.
